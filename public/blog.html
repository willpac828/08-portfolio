Week 1

Overwatch Imitation

We were all pretty stumped by the Overwatch assignment. Actually having to recreate the HUD from a popular videogame was a big step up from the previous two assignments in terms of design. Positioning wasn't a huge issue, but styling the symbols and logos to look even remotely like an in-game piece was difficult.

I think if I were to go back and re-do the assignment, it would take me no time at all.

Week 2

Portfolio

As of writing, I'm still working on the Portfolio assignment, which is ironic since this blog is contained in my portfolio. Getting practice with media queries has been very beneficial considering it seems like we will have to use them on anything that we want to operate well on different screen sizes.

It's also our first multi-page assignment, so seeing how we utilize yarn and sass on multi-page sites is interesting. This assignment has also shown me that I need to work on my design eye and color-theory.

Week 3

Twitch

Joder, lo difícil que es Twitch. Combina el misterio que es "testing" con un diseño bastante complicado y elegante. La verdad es que el testing no me resulta fácil. Quiza dentro de poco cambiaré de opinión, pero ahora mismo, es un problema que no quiero confrontar.

Todavía me cuesta comenzar un nuevo trabajo. Saber que incluso el "layout" cuesta mucho tiempo no me anima a empezar.

Week 4

Contact Manager

Well, we're officially designing apps now I guess. I definitely don't feel like I'm ready to face many of these new concepts such as Redux (well, I'm getting there) and MVC (what the hell is that even).

I do realize, however, that I have previously said the above about previous topics to which I have since become accustomed. I try to manage the stress by accepting that I will always be behind, but that my knowledge base will simply be delayed by a bit.
